Paper Reading Essay 4

Author: Yugeng Liu Date: August 27, 2020  12:23:54 Category: Papers

In the paper reading group, I present a paper named An Empirical Study on the Robustness of NAS based Architectures. Generally speaking, It is easy to understand this paper.

Summary

this paper finds most existing methods for Neural Architecture Search (NAS) lack robustness on large datasets, which have not been attempted and that the number of parameters co-relates to robustness within the same family of architectures.

Proposed Questions

The authors propose three questions in the paper and answer them by using the experiments. The questions are as follows.

How do NAS-based methods compare with handcrafted models in terms of robustness?
How does the robustness of NAS based architectures vary concerning the dataset size?
Does an increase in the number of parameters of NAS-based architectures help improve robustness?

Experiments and Conclusions

For the first question, the authors test the handcraft models and NAS based architectures on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and Imagenet. They use some adversarial attack dataset to evaluate the robustness. there are three tables.



From the tables, in the case of CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, we can get NSGA-Net outperforms every other architecture in terms of architectural robustness for attacks but handcrafted models like Densenets are more robust than NAS-based methods on Imagenet.

For the second question, Figure 1 shows the difference between the maximum accuracy of NAS based architectures and the maximum accuracy of handcrafted models for all the four attacks shown in Tables 1, 2, and 3. From the figure, in general, as the dataset scale increases, the robustness of NAS-based methods decreases when compared with handcrafted models.


In the third question, the authors run experiments on two families of DenseNAS, models A, B, C, and Large which use MobileNetV2-based search space and R1, R2, R3 which use ResNet-based search space. From the figure 2 and table 4, the authors conclude that increasing the parameters of a NAS based architecture can improve its robustness albeit it comes with an increase in training and inference time.
